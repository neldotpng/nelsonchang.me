Wanderlust

UX / UI Design

Trunk Club Designation

Wanderlust is a mobile app that functions like a mixture of Yelp and Pinterest for traveling. Our client had us explore the picture and location sharing aspect of the app. The primary function of the picture and location sharing portion of the app was for users to be able to share and find travel inspiration.

Skills

Competitive Research, Paper Prototyping, User Testing, Wireframing, Mobile App Design, InVision

Deliverables

Wireframes, Personas, App Screen Designs, Low-Mid Fidelity Prototype

App Exploration

Wanderlust was intended to have three primary functionalities that would comprise the overall travel app.

  • Travel blogging / photo sharing and a feature allowing users to search and sort through user submissions
  • Local recommendations, a feature that would allow users to explore areas and activities recommended by locals
  • A review functionality that would allow users to submit and explore other user reviews of locations and activities

Having only enough time to fully explore one of the options, we opted for the photo and location sharing service.

Competitive Research

Given the nature of a photo and location sharing aspect of the app, we chose to explore two of the foremost photo and blogging apps: Instagram and Tumblr. We also explored several more travel-oriented apps: Travelog, Yonder, TripMayor, and Everplaces.

Synthesis

One common feature that we discovered between travel apps was that they were built like social media apps, with liking and commenting being the foremost features available to users. We wanted to steer away from designing Wanderlust as another social media app, so we aimed to design Wanderlust without an explicit like or like tracking feature.

Another key feature that we wanted to include was the ability to include multiple photos in a single post. Of the apps we looked into, only Travelog and Tumblr allowed for posts with multiple photos.

The last feature that we wanted to include to set apart Wanderlust's photo sharing content was an Editor's Choice section, which would include hand-picked, high quality, user-submitted content. We found that content on the home or popular pages were often of low quality.

Personas

We determined the target audience of our app to be relatively young, financially-independent individuals. We further centered our app design around individuals that were not tied down by family and were more likely to pursue adventurous travels.

Sketches + Wireframes

After establishing our personas, I began sketching and exploring designs. My initial sketches focused on the layout of content and the post submission process. The sketched screens can be viewed in my paper prototype on POP.

Using my paper prototype, we ran some brief user testing on the photo submission aspect of our app, as well as on a new random results feature which we labeled as 'Wander.' The purpose of our user test was to be sure that both the process and the wording were clear and concise.

Based on our sketches and user feedback, we created initial wireframes for the main screens of the app. In our first wireframes, we utilized a 2-level navigation bar at the top of the app that would partially hide when not in use. In my final design, I ended up using a bottom-positioned chrome for navigation.
